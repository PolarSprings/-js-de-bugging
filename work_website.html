WEBSITE

Task

Lead the content effort of a global website revamp, with an emphasis on SEO and UX.

Context

Current Situation

A UX writing initiative uncovered questions about how we explained our offerings to clients. Answers led to a global website redesign.

Business Goal

Modernize the website with a new design. Design a scalable solution that considers new offices, languages, and markets.

Content Goal

Elevate the blog, update SEO strategy, and implement a tagging system to help users navigate the site. Set and track KPIs.

Solution

We launched a global website, labbrand.com. The new site featured a cleaner navigation, integrated website tags, and auto-redirect for 5 major markets.

Process

A challenging step was aligning team members - including designers and technologists - who had other work to prioritize. To ensure efficency, I led a full content audit with data from Google Analytics; after tagging all pages and setting a guideline, team members broke off and completed the audit on their own time.

Prioritizing content created depth

Coming together with three classes - rewrite, remove, keep - we prioritized top-performing content. We also considered where new users entered the site from; with over half of users entering the site through a search results page, we focused on this as the main point of entry in mapping a typical user's journey.

Establishing a coherent plan with regards to SEO involved a good amount of trial and error. New keywords were categorized by items like search volume and brand fit, assigned a quantitative score, organized into one of the priority classes and aggregated into a spreadsheet.

As a clear benefit, this information was available not only during UX writing, but also as applied to our broader editorial strategy. We could now reference items like headers, subheaders, and meta descriptions with a more cohesive digital vision in mind.

Writing for the user

Inputing the framework was next. This involved meeting together with consulting departments, marketing managers, and key stakeholders; many, many meetings. But the result was exhilerating - two nav buttons folded into one About page, which hosted a model intro to the company as a pioneer in brand innovations. We expanded this style throughout the site.

The framework supported an updated blog structure, turning three seperate pages into one knowledge hub, tags to help the user navigate.

With tags assigned and a clear picture of the website direction, the designers began building hi-fidelity wireframes and I began writing the website copy - an in-depth collaboration with subject matter experts.

On-track and on-time, I set KPIs for traffic, pages per session, bounce rate, and returning visitors. The team handed off all written and visual content to our technology lead to be turned into code.

Results

We succesfully launched a new global website with simplified navigation, stronger user journey, and new knowledge center.
A number to be proud of!
71%
  • Under 40% bounce rate from 80%, below the industry average
  • Amount of time on page increased significantly through the new website structure
  • Conversion points were set at the knowledge center entrance, and reached 71% over the next half year - an increase of 129%
  • Returning visitors began growing steadily, passing our KPI (21%) after one month and reaching a 38% increase over the next half year
